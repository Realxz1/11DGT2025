high card

  • The worst hand you could ever get
  • no matching pairs
  • Extremly low chance of winning
  • An ace of hearts (the high card), and 7 of clubs

one pair

  • An ideal hand but has mid win-rate
  • The higher the pair, the better
  • A pair doesn't need to have matching suits
  • Two cards of the same number is a pair

two pair

  • The idealest of ideal to win without geting too lucky
  • Beats a pair, slightly higher chance of winning
  • Two pairs of the same number

three of a kind

  • Solid chance of winning
  • Beats two pairs and below
  • 3 cards of the same number, no matter the suits, is a three of a kind

straight

  • Beats three of a kind and below
  • Quite rare to get
  • A minimum of 5 sequencing numbers no matter the suits

flush

  • Beats a straight and below
  • slightly rarer than straight
  • A minimum of 5 cards with the same suits

full house

  • Beats a flush and below
  • A solid hand, good winrate
  • A pair and a three of a kind is a full house

four of a kind

  • Beats a full house and below
  • A very good hand, very good winrate
  • 4 cards of the same number is a quad (four of a kind)

straight flush

  • Beats a four of a kind and below
  • The second rarest hand anyone could possibly get
  • Mostly guaranteed to win
  • A minimum of 5 cards with the same suits and number is a straight flush

Royal Flush

  • The Best hand in the game
  • Only 1 in 30,000 people can get dealt this hand
  • A sequence of Ace, King, Queen, Jack, and 10 all of the same suits
