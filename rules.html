Brief Overview
Texas Hold Em' is a poker variant that is hands down the most popular variant played throughout western culture. It was established sometime during the early 1900’s but blew up in the 2000's due to its exposure to television. Since then, Texas Hold Em' has been the most common gambling game to play in U.S casinos. This website page will teach you new-coming gamblers and non-gamblers the basic rules and a rundown of Poker Texas Hold Em'. Keep in mind that when playing poker, you exchange your cash into chips to bet on. If you want to learn how to play poker for fun, read only the sentences that are bold as well as 'Betting Rounds' and 'General tips to consider'.

Rules
In a casino, there are nine positions around the table and a person will have a dealer button beside them (meaning they’ll shuffle and deal the cards out), and the two players to the dealer’s left side are forced to bet blindly. The person in the ‘Big Blind’ position posts the current minimum bet, while the person in the ‘Small Blind’ bets half the amount. The dealer would then hand out two cards (going clockwise, starting at the left side of the dealer’s button), and three cards known as ‘community cards’ (cards shared by all the players) in the middle. During the pre-flop round, the players have the choice to ‘call’, ‘raise’, or ‘fold’. The player in the 'Big Blind' doesn't need to bet again, but the player in the 'Small Blind' needs to bet another half of the current minimum bet in order to continue playing. After the three community cards are revealed, players have an extra option to say ‘check’, only doable after the pre-flop round. The goal of poker is to have the best hand by the end of round to win the prize pool.

call
It means to match the bet someone else has made. When you call, you're adding extra money equal to the amount of the bet your matching to the betting pool.

raise and check
'Raise' means to add more money to the betting pool. Meanwhile, 'Check' means to avoid betting but continue playing.

fold
It means if someone made a bet and you don't want to match it. When you fold, turn your cards face-down to your dealer. In other words, if you choose to fold, then you withdraw from the round.

Betting Rounds A.K.A Flops
There is a total of 4 rounds in a game of poker. The pre-flop round is the first round that introduces three community cards face-down. The second round flips the community cards face-up and allows the players around the table to make their choice. Then the third round introduces an extra community card to the table (face-up). The last round will then add one last community card making a total of 5 face-up community cards for the players to share. The dealer would then ask the remaining players to reveal their hand, whoever has the best hand wins the prize pool (if playing in a casino).

General tips to consider

  • Fold as early as you can if you know you can't win, never give your opponents an advantage
  • To bluff, you must act confident and keep a poker face, if you bluff too much, you become predictable
  • If you have been dealt a high pair (pocket aces) early on, don't get greedy
  • PRO TIP: A game of poker will only end until one player wins all the chips, use this to your advanage to read the behaviours of your opponents. Reading your opponent's behaviour gives you the upper hand. You'll learn eventually.
